### What is offshoring and outsourcing?
Offshoring and outsourcing is assigning or consigning some aspects of business operations to a service provider in another country. Businesses of any size can opt to engage in offshoring or outsourcing with the intent to reduce cost, streamline processes, increase efficiency and productivity, giving them the opportunity to focus on other equally important parts of their businesses.

Offshoring or outsourcing to the Philippines has become a well-accepted business strategy given the country's outstanding track record, business recognition and significant contribution to the global industry. And as more and more countries around the globe continue to consider the Philippines as a foremost country for offshoring and outsourcing, this industry will continue to grow and expand.

### Industries supported

### What are front-office services?
Front-office outsourcing and offshoring (widely known as call center services) is what the country was initially known for. The BPO industry has evolved to Contact Center as a Service (CCaS), providing a wider platform of support to its customers.

Some of the commonly outsourced contact center services include:
- **Customer Service** provides a platform to field in customer inquiries for a product or service, processes orders, or resolves customer issues.
- **Technical Support** focuses on resolving technical issues related to a product or service encountered by customers.
- **Sales and Telemarketing** is where existing or potential customers are contacted to sell a product or service.
- **Billing and Collections** support businesses by handling billing, invoicing, and collection activities.

### What is back-office outsourcing?
A back-office can be compared to being the back-bone of company operations. This is where human resources management, payroll, finance and accounting, information technology management, and data entry are performed. Most of these services require limited to no direct customer interaction and can be outsourced or offshored.

A strong and solid back office often results in quality improvement. It also supports companies by allowing companies to optimize their resources to pursue growth opportunities, develop their competencies further, and improve their business strategies.

Some of the commonly outsourced back-office functions include:
- **Human Resources** helps companies design better programs and strategies in HR areas like leave management, total rewards, recruitment and talent management, performance consulting, and other HR services.
- **Data Mining and Analytics** unlocks the power of big data to get insights on customers, operations, and business activities to help with strategies.
- **IT Support** handles technical issues related to product, warranty, minor or major technical issues, or IT infrastructure support that results in higher customer satisfaction or retention when resolved.
- **Finance and Accounting** provides income tax, cash flow, accounts payable management, and accounts receivable support with accuracy and efficiency.
- **Data Entry** services allows companies to encode and process large data logs with high levels of accuracy, efficiency, and cost effectiveness.

## RESOURCES — HOW TO GET STARTED

### Discover

**Assess and understand business requirements**
Before starting your outsourcing / offshoring journey, it is critical for a company to establish the business goals they need to achieve and to choose the right partner to help them drive towards success. During the Discovery phase, the right partner will work with you in understanding your processes, your pain points and your needs to be able to assess readiness and success. An effective Discovery process carefully assesses goals, objectives, current and planned business models, and identify stakeholders. This results in risk reduction, a clear road map for implementation and establish confidence in the project. During the Discovery period, the right partner should be able to fill the gaps, answer discovery questions, and create a clear vision for the business. The right partner should be able to prepare an in-depth assessment and documentation. This will pave the way for clear and mutually agreed goals in mind that sets the tone on what needs to be accomplished by your company and the right partner.

**Calibrate**
Another essential process in the discovery phase is calibrate. Calibration allows your company and the right partner to align with your goals and objectives. Using this opportunity to calibrate not only identifies opportunities and road blocks but it defines what are the measures for success of the project. Calibrating is also the perfect opportunity to do a deep dive on your business and understand each of the following areas – people, process, technology, and even culture! Successfully completing this analysis sets up the project more towards success.

**Establish Baseline**
Establishing baseline is necessary in completing the assessment and calibration, in order to align business challenges, opportunities, goals, processes and available solutions. The right partner will help your company establish the baselines that is key to ensuring a successful discovery phase up to project implementation. Meanwhile, a comprehensive documentation will be essential in establishing a system of compliance and governance.

### Design
The design phase is just as crucial as any other step in your outsourcing or offshoring journey. This is where we do the process modeling of your existing and proposed frameworks, based on a thorough analysis done during the discovery phase. By simulating both your current and our recommended frameworks, we will be able to minimize risks and standardize your processes, resulting in a streamlined and cohesive approach. Don't worry if we need to recalibrate and re-align along the way — that's all part of the process! To make sure we cover all our bases, this phase should encompass everything from process and technology to people, organization, and culture.

### Deliver
The final part of your outsourcing/offshoring journey is the delivery phase — the moment we've all been waiting for! Typically, it kicks off with a meeting of all stakeholders to ensure everyone is on the same page and ready to move forward. To make sure everything runs smoothly, we need to have a few things in place:

**Establish Service Level Agreements (SLAs)** that set out performance measures and goals. We'll compare these against what we consider to be the glide path, so we can make sure we're on track.

**Adapt a collaborative model** where communication is key. We'll be constantly touching base, handing over tasks, aligning our efforts, and calibrating our approach. While we'll have clear process accountability and ownership, we'll always prioritize a collaborative and synergized approach.

**Establish and monitor controls** by setting up processes to ensure that everything is running smoothly. We need to keep an eye on **risk and quality monitoring**. We'll conduct frequent and regular audits and checks to ensure that we're meeting our SLAs and that our goals remain accurate, relevant, realistic, and achievable.

With all these measures in place, we're ready to power your business strategies.

### 2. Offshoring and Outsourcing. What's the difference?
Although they share a common goal of employing the best talent to push for your business agenda, offshoring and outsourcing are two different strategies:

**Offshoring**
- You can select your own qualified team members based on your skill requirements and job description.
- Team members will follow your processes and procedures.
- Have direct control over team members' performance and compensation.

**Outsourcing**
- Team members are hired by your provider based on the qualifications and skills agreed upon to perform the outsourced work.
- The provider may recommend enhancements in your processes and procedures.
- The outsourcing company manages staff performance and productivity to ensure that committed client service levels are met.

### 3. What are the benefits of offshoring and outsourcing?
